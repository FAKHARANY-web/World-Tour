The Pyramids of Giza


The Egyptian pyramids are monumental tombs built for pharaohs during the Old and Middle Kingdoms. The Great Pyramid of Giza is the last standing Wonder of the Ancient World.

Key Facts

  • Oldest: Step Pyramid of Djoser (c. 2670 BCE).
  • Most famous: The Great Pyramid of Khufu.
  • Over 130 pyramids have been discovered in Egypt.
  • Purpose: Tombs to ensure the king’s afterlife.
  • Construction: Took around 10–20 years for the Great Pyramid.

The Nile River


The Nile River is the longest river in the world and the lifeline of Ancient Egypt, providing fertile land and enabling civilization to flourish for thousands of years.

Key Facts

  • Length: About 6,650 km.
  • Source: White Nile & Blue Nile.
  • Importance: Annual flooding fertilized farmlands.
  • Modern project: Aswan High Dam (1970).
  • Historical canals: Suez Canal & Mahmoudiyah Canal.

The Tomb of Tutankhamun


The Tomb of Tutankhamun is one of the most famous archaeological discoveries in the world, located in the Valley of the Kings near Luxor, Egypt. It was discovered in 1922 by British archaeologist Howard Carter and is the final resting place of the young Pharaoh Tutankhamun, who ruled during the 18th Dynasty.

Key Facts

  • Location: Valley of the Kings, Luxor, Egypt.
  • Pharaoh: Tutankhamun (ruled around 1332-1323 BCE).
  • Discovery: Found in 1922 by British archaeologist Howard Carter.
  • Attractions: The tomb itself, the golden burial mask, and the many treasures discovered inside.
  • History: The tomb was nearly intact with over 5,000 artifacts, providing valuable insight into ancient Egyptian burial practices.
  • Famous Event: The discovery of the tomb and its treasures, which revealed a wealth of information about ancient Egypt’s art, culture, and royal life.

Siwa Oasis


Siwa is one of Egypt’s most unique and beautiful oases, famous for its salt lakes, palm groves, Berber culture, and the ancient Temple of Amun visited by Alexander the Great.

Key Facts

  • Location: Western Desert near Libya.
  • Culture: Home to the Berber-speaking Siwi people.
  • Attractions: Salt lakes, Cleopatra’s Spring, Shali Fortress.
  • History: Human settlement since 10,000 BCE.
  • Famous event: Alexander the Great’s visit to the Oracle.

Ras Mohamed Nature Reserve


Ras Mohamed Nature Reserve is one of Egypt's most renowned and diverse nature reserves, celebrated for its vibrant marine life, stunning coral reefs, and unique desert landscapes.

Key Facts

  • Location: Southern Sinai Peninsula, at the meeting point of the Gulf of Suez and Gulf of Aqaba.
  • Wildlife: Home to rare species of birds, marine life, and desert animals like gazelles and ibexes.
  • Attractions: Coral reefs, beaches, mangrove forests, and the famous Shark Reef and Yolanda Reef dive sites.
  • History: Established as a protected area in 1983; historically used by the Pharaohs for trade.
  • Famous event: Popular for its role in eco-tourism and scuba diving tourism, attracting visitors worldwide.
